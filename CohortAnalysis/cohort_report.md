# Customer Cohort & Retention Analysis - Technical Report

## 1. Executive Summary

This report details the methodology, technical design, and key findings of the **Customer Cohort & Retention Analysis** conducted for the **Restaurant Menu Engineering and Profitability Analysis** chain.

Customer retention and behavior were tracked longitudinally using transaction history spanning **36 consecutive months** (2022-01-01 to 2024-12-31) representing **3,000 unique customers**. Customers are grouped into monthly cohorts based on their acquisition (first purchase) date.

### Key Cohort Metrics:
* **Total Customers Audited**: **3,000 acquired customers**
* **Month 1 Average Retention (Day-30)**: **50.6%** (Initial drop-off: **49.4%**)
* **Month 12 Average Retention (Day-360)**: **41.3%**
* **Average 12-Month Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**: **Rs. 24,153.75** cumulative spend per acquired customer.
* **Peak Retention Performance**: Cohorts acquired during the holiday seasons (November/December) show a **+8.4%** average retention improvement compared to summer cohorts.

---

## 2. Cohort Definition & Mathematical Formulation

### A. Acquisition Cohorts
Customers are assigned to monthly acquisition cohorts based on their earliest transaction timestamp in the sales logs:
$$\text{Acquisition Date}_c = \min_{t \in \text{Sales}_c} (\text{order\_date}_t)$$
$$\text{Cohort Month}_c = \text{to\_period\_month}(\text{Acquisition Date}_c)$$

### B. Cohort Index
For any subsequent transaction $t$ made by customer $c$, the Cohort Index $i \ge 0$ represents the elapsed months since their acquisition:
$$\text{Cohort Index}_{t} = (\text{Year}_{t} - \text{Year}_{\text{acq},c}) \times 12 + (\text{Month}_{t} - \text{Month}_{\text{acq},c})$$
* **Month 0** represents the acquisition month (where retention is by definition 100.0%).

### C. Cohort Metrics Formulae
* **Retention Rate ($R_{m, i}$)**: The percentage of customers from cohort $m$ who made at least one purchase in index month $i$:
  $$R_{m, i} = \frac{\text{Unique active customers in cohort } m \text{ at index } i}{\text{Total customers in cohort } m \text{ (Cohort Size at index 0)}} \times 100$$
* **Average Spend per Active Customer ($S_{m, i}$)**: The average net sales generated by active cohort members in index month $i$:
  $$S_{m, i} = \frac{\text{Total Net Sales generated by cohort } m \text{ in index month } i}{\text{Unique active customers in cohort } m \text{ at index } i}$$
* **Cumulative Lifetime Value ($\text{LTV}_{m, i}$)**: The cumulative net revenue contributed per acquired customer in cohort $m$ up to index month $i$:
  $$\text{LTV}_{m, i} = \frac{\sum_{j=0}^{i} \text{Total Net Sales generated by cohort } m \text{ in index month } j}{\text{Total customers in cohort } m}$$

## 3. Retention Decay & Drop-off Analysis

### A. The "Month 1 Drop-off" (The Leaky Bucket)
The chain experiences a significant retention drop-off immediately after acquisition:
* Average Month 1 retention across all cohorts is **50.6%**.
* This means **49.4%** of newly acquired customers do not return in their first month.
* *Insight*: This initial drop-off represents the primary revenue leakage point. High acquisition marketing spend is wasted if half of the acquired customers never return.

### B. Long-Term Stabilization
After the initial Month 1 drop, retention curves stabilize:
* **Month 2 Retention**: **47.5%**
* **Month 6 Retention**: **44.8%**
* **Month 12 Retention**: **41.3%**
* **Month 24 Retention**: **38.6%**
* This flat decay curve indicates that customers who survive the first month become highly loyal, repeat diners with very low subsequent churn.

---

## 4. Lifetime Value (LTV) Trajectories

The cumulative spending per acquired customer grows steadily over time, highlighting the compounding value of customer retention:
* **Month 0 LTV**: **Rs. 5,023.45** (Average first-visit check size)
* **Month 3 LTV**: **Rs. 10,230.15**
* **Month 6 LTV**: **Rs. 15,112.50**
* **Month 12 LTV**: **Rs. 24,153.75**
* **Month 24 LTV**: **Rs. 42,084.10**

*LTV / CAC Implications*: If Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is estimated at Rs. 2,000 (e.g. discount vouchers, local advertising), the payback period is achieved in Month 0. By Month 12, the LTV/CAC ratio is **12.1x**, indicating highly profitable acquisition dynamics if retention is maintained.

---

## 5. Segmented Cohort Dynamics

Segmenting the cohorts by customer attributes reveals stark operational deviations:

### A. Loyalty Program Impact (Members vs. Non-Members)
* **Members**: Average Month 12 retention is **64.2%**.
* **Non-Members (Guests)**: Average Month 12 retention drops to **18.4%**.
* *Insight*: The loyalty program is highly effective at driving retention. Members return at a rate $3.5\times$ higher than non-members.

### B. Branch and City Performance
* **Suburb Cohorts (Suburb branch)**: Show exceptionally stable retention (Month 12 retention: **48.5%**).
* **Downtown Cohorts (Downtown branch)**: Show higher Month 1 retention (**71.5%**) due to initial novelty, but steep subsequent decay (Month 12 retention: **32.4%**).

---

## 6. Strategic Business Recommendations

1. **Focus on the Month 1 Return Visit**:
   Since the biggest churn occurs between Month 0 and Month 1 (49.4% drop-off), implement an automated POS email sequence: send a **"Second Visit Discount"** voucher (valid for 14 days) to every new guest immediately after their first purchase.
2. **First-Visit Menu Optimization**:
   Analysis shows that customers who order a "Star" menu item (e.g. *Lamb Biryani*) on their first visit exhibit a **+14% higher lifetime retention** than those who order "Dog" items. Waitstaff should actively recommend Star items to first-time diners.
3. **Expand Loyalty Program Sign-ups**:
   Since Loyalty Members retain at 64.2% (vs. 18.4% for non-members), POS agents should actively prompt guests to sign up for the loyalty program. Consider offering a free dessert or appetizer on their next visit as a signup incentive.
